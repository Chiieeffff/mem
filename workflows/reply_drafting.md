# Reply Drafting — Workflow

## Objective

Given an incoming message (LinkedIn DM or Telegram) and brief context, produce two reply options — one short/direct, one more detailed — with a clear recommendation on which to send.

---

## Required inputs

- **Message**: The full text of the incoming message (paste verbatim)
- **Context**: 1–3 lines covering:
  - Who sent it (name, role, company)
  - Relationship type: cold lead / warm lead / active partner / team member / other
  - Stage: first reply to outreach / inbound interest / ongoing negotiation / team coordination / other
  - Any relevant history (optional — include if it changes the tone)

If context is missing, ask once before writing.

---

## Step 1 — Classify the message

Before drafting, identify:

| Field | What to determine |
|---|---|
| Tone required | Formal / semi-formal / casual |
| Intent of sender | Interest / objection / question / update / request |
| Urgency | Needs same-day reply / can wait / no deadline |
| Relationship stage | Cold → warm → active → post-deal |

---

## Step 2 — Draft two replies

### Variant A — Short & direct
- 2–4 sentences maximum
- Moves the conversation forward with one clear next step
- No filler ("Thanks for reaching out", "Hope this finds you well", etc.)
- Recommended when: relationship is warm, intent is clear, context is established

### Variant B — More detailed
- 4–8 sentences
- Adds a bit more context, proof, or framing before the ask
- Still no filler — every sentence earns its place
- Recommended when: cold lead, first substantive reply, complex question, or objection that needs addressing

---

## Step 3 — Output format

```
**Context read:** [1-line summary of what you understood — confirm before reading the drafts]

---

**Variant A — Short (Recommended / Not recommended)**
[message]

---

**Variant B — Detailed (Recommended / Not recommended)**
[message]

---

**Recommendation:** [A or B] — [one sentence on why]
```

---

## Tone rules (always apply)

- Write in first person as Roman
- No corporate hedging: no "just checking in", no "I wanted to reach out", no "as per my last message"
- No passive voice where active works
- Match the sender's register — if they wrote casually, don't write formally
- For GatewayCrypto leads: do not overpitch in replies. Replies should advance the conversation, not restart the pitch.
- For team messages: be direct, no sign-off formalities needed

---

## Anti-patterns

- Do not open with "Thanks for your message" or any variation
- Do not use "I hope this helps" as a closing
- Do not ask "Are you the right person to discuss this?" — if they replied, they're the right person
- Do not include product feature lists in reply drafts unless the sender specifically asked about features
- Do not write "Let me know if you have any questions" — it's filler; replace with a specific next step
