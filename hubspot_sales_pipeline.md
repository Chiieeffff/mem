GatewayCrypto — HubSpot Sales Pipeline


IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE

B2B merchants and platforms with real transaction volume that need to accept crypto payments, convert crypto to fiat, or both.

Minimum bar to qualify:
- Operates a business that processes payments (not an individual)
- Has meaningful transaction volume (low turnover is a disqualifier)
- Can pass KYB
- Has a genuine use case for crypto acceptance, off-ramp, or both


LEAD SOURCES

- LinkedIn — responded to outreach, showed genuine interest
- Own — sourced directly, outside LinkedIn
- Inbound — reached out to us first
- Referral — introduced by a partner or existing client


PIPELINE STAGES


1. Lead Stack

Raw, unworked leads sitting in the queue. No evaluation or outreach has happened yet.

- Entry: Lead added to HubSpot via any source
- Exit: Lead has been opened and reviewed
- Trigger: You pull and begin evaluating it — move to Qualification


2. Qualification

Actively being evaluated for ICP fit. No outreach made yet — internal assessment only.

What you're checking:
- Meaningful transaction volume?
- Clear use case for crypto acceptance or off-ramp?
- Likely to pass KYB?
- A real business, not an individual?

- Entry: Lead pulled from Lead Stack
- Exit: ICP decision made
- Trigger: Qualifies — move to MQL | Off-target — move to Lost (Off Target or Low Turnover)


3. MQL — Marketing Qualified Lead

Passed qualification. Initial contact made or intent shown — waiting on real engagement.

- Entry: ICP confirmed + one of: outreach sent / inbound received / LinkedIn reply
- Exit: Meaningful reply showing real interest (not a courtesy response)
- Trigger: Genuine engagement — move to SQL | No response after full sequence — move to Lost


4. SQL — Sales Qualified Lead

Engaged and ready for a sales conversation. Real back-and-forth is live.

- Entry: Meaningful two-way dialogue — questions, interest, or call agreed
- Exit: Demo done + strong fit confirmed + clear timeline (all three required)
- Trigger: All three met — move to Pushing | Goes cold or disqualifies — move to Lost


5. Pushing

Demo done. Active deal in motion — driving toward close.

Activities:
- Pricing and commercial terms
- KYB submission and review
- Integration scoping (API or Backoffice)
- Legal and agreement sign-off

- Entry: Demo done + fit confirmed + timeline real
- Exit: Contract signed + onboarding started, or deal collapses
- Trigger: Signed and live — move to Client | Collapses — move to Lost (reason required)


6. Client

Closed and onboarded. Merchant is live on GatewayCrypto. Terminal stage.

- Entry: Agreement signed + onboarding complete (API integrated or Backoffice active)
- Exit: N/A
- Trigger: Onboarding confirmed live — move to Client


7. Lost

Disqualified or dropped at any stage. Loss reason must be logged. May be reactivated manually if circumstances change.

- Entry: Deal inactive or disqualified, loss reason logged
- Exit: N/A

Loss reasons:
- Price — rates or fees don't work
- In-house solution — building or already built their own processing
- External solution — chose a competitor
- Missing feature/functionality — GWC can't support their requirement
- Refused/Failed KYB — did not pass compliance
- Low turnover — volume too low to make sense commercially
- Off target — wrong profile, never should have been in the pipeline


STAGE FLOW

Lead Stack > Qualification > MQL > SQL > Pushing > Client

Every stage can exit to Lost. Reason required.
