MOCK CALL SCENARIO — GATEWAY CRYPTO SALE
Prospect: Operator on Softgamings platform
Your goal: Qualify + pitch Gateway Crypto as their crypto processing solution
=============================================================================


BEFORE THE CALL — KNOW THIS
-----------------------------
- They're already running a gaming operation on Softgamings, so they have volume
- They likely have some crypto processing already, or they've been asked about it
  by their players and are now shopping around
- Your angle: you know their stack (Softgamings), you know their world,
  and you have a solution that actually fits it
- Keep the call to 20-30 min. Qualify first, pitch second, close on next step.


OPENING (first 2 minutes)
--------------------------
You: "Hey [Name], thanks for making time. So you're running on Softgamings —
good setup. I wanted to jump on a quick call because we work with a number of
operators in the iGaming space and I think there might be a fit here with
Gateway Crypto. But before I go into anything, let me just ask — what's your
current situation with crypto payments? Are you processing at all, or is this
something you're looking to add?"

[Let them talk. This tells you everything.]


QUALIFY — LISTEN FOR THESE
---------------------------
Good signals:
- "We have crypto but it's painful / expensive / unreliable"
- "Our players ask for it but we haven't set it up yet"
- "We're paying X% and want to reduce fees"
- "We've had issues with withdrawals failing or being slow"
- "Our current provider doesn't support [chain/currency]"

Red flags:
- They mention US player base as primary geo
- Very low volumes (<$75K/month) with no clear growth path
- They want to "just try it with no commitment" but have no real urgency


DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (pick 2-3, don't interrogate)
--------------------------------------------------
- "What geos are you focused on? Where do most of your players come from?"
- "Are you currently processing crypto or is this new for you?"
- "What currencies do your players use most — USDT, BTC, others?"
- "Have you run into any issues with your current provider — chain-specific
  problems, failed withdrawals, slow settlement?"
- "What kind of monthly volumes are we talking roughly?"


THE PITCH — LEAD WITH THE PROBLEM THEY JUST DESCRIBED
------------------------------------------------------
Don't recite features. Mirror back what they told you, then connect it.

If they mentioned withdrawal issues:
  "So that's actually exactly the problem Gateway solves at the root level.
   Most processors handle USDT on a specific chain — TRC20 or ERC20 — and when
   liquidity runs out on that chain, withdrawals fail. It's a 4am problem.
   Gateway is network-agnostic — it doesn't matter which chain the USDT is on,
   it handles it automatically. No cross-chain swap fees, no manual ops,
   no dry liquidity. For a gaming operator, that's the difference between
   players getting paid and players charging back."

If they're shopping on price:
  "Before we talk numbers — what are you quoting right now? Because most
   operators come to us saying 0.4%, and that's usually just the deposit fee.
   The full-cycle cost — deposits, auto-exchanges, withdrawals with conversion —
   is almost always higher than they think. We build pricing around the full
   picture so there are no surprises."

If they're new to crypto:
  "Good, then you're not stuck with bad habits from another provider.
   Gateway can get you live in under 24 hours — KYB and integration run in
   parallel so you're not waiting around. You'd have a dedicated account manager
   and 24/7 integration support. For a first crypto setup, that matters a lot."


THE TRUST BUILDERS (drop 1-2 naturally, not all at once)
---------------------------------------------------------
- "18 months, zero merchant churn. Nobody who's started with us has left
   for another provider. In this space, that says something."
- "We've never been hacked. 99.9% uptime. You can't prove security until
  something goes wrong — but nothing has."
- "Your players can do a simplified KYC on first deposit and then transact
  freely up to $10K after that. Frictionless for the player, clean for you."


HANDLING COMMON OBJECTIONS
---------------------------

"We already have a crypto processor."
→ "What's your biggest frustration with them right now?"
  [Wait. There's always something. Then position Gateway against that specific thing.]

"It sounds expensive."
→ "What are you paying now, full cycle? Deposit, exchange, withdrawal?
   Most operators are surprised when they add it up. Let's compare apples
   to apples before we decide it's expensive."

"We need to think about it / talk to the team."
→ "Of course. What specifically do you need to get clarity on?
   I can get you a one-pager on the product and we can do a quick demo —
   usually that's enough to make a decision either way. What's your timeline?"

"We're not sure about compliance / KYB process."
→ "Totally fair question. They do practical due diligence — not just paperwork.
   They work with high-risk operators directly, no need for storefronts or
   workarounds. The KYB doc is one page. I can get it to you today."

"What about geos — we have players from [country]?"
→ Handle anything except USA retail-facing and China. For edge cases
  (Russian passport holders in EU entities, etc.) — flag it but don't kill
  the deal. Say "let me confirm that specific case with the team, it's usually
  fine but I want to give you the right answer."


THE CLOSE — ALWAYS CLOSE ON A NEXT STEP, NOT A DECISION
--------------------------------------------------------
Don't ask "do you want to sign up?" Ask for the next concrete action.

Option A — They're warm:
  "Here's what I'd suggest — let me send you the one-pager today, and let's
   set up a 20-minute demo with the Gateway team this week. You'll see the
   product, ask your questions, and you'll know quickly if it's the right fit.
   Does Thursday or Friday work?"

Option B — They need internal alignment:
  "Makes sense. What I can do is send you the one-pager and a quick summary
   of pricing so you have something concrete to share with your team.
   Who else needs to be in the room for a decision like this?"

Option C — They're ready now:
  "Perfect. Here's how it works — I'll intro you to the Gateway team on
   Telegram. You send them your business type, geos, and what you need.
   They pre-approve quickly and then set up a group to handle everything:
   KYB, integration, pricing. You could be live inside a week."
